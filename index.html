About Me

Hi! I'm Mercy Omwoyo a Computer Science graduate student passionate about blending technology and education to unlock new possibilities in learning. My fascination lies in understanding systems ranging from algorithms to user behaviors.

Software Engineering

Building systems and elegant solutions through distributed systems and machine learning algorithms.

Design

Crafting intuitive user experiences and transforming complex technical problems into accessible solutions.

Research

Exploring educational technologies and investigating ways to make digital experiences more inclusive and effective.

From designing intuitive user interfaces to implementing robust backend systems, I thrive at the intersection of creativity and engineering. Through research and practical projects, I've worked to make computer science education more engaging and accessible, especially for underrepresented groups. My goal is to bridge the gap between theoretical innovation and practical solutions that make a tangible impact in classrooms and beyond.

Mission Statement: to use the power of technology to democratize education and inspire the next generation of learners and creators.

Beyond Code

Kenyan Roots

Growing up in Kenya shaped my perspective on technology's role in bridging cultural and educational gaps. From sailing along the pristine coastal beaches to seeing wildlife in national parks, these experiences fuel my creative approach to problem-solving.

Adventure Seeker

As a former member of the President's Award Club in Kenya (part of the Duke of Edinburgh's International Award), I developed a passion for exploration through challenging expeditions. Today, I continue this adventure by hiking mountainous trails with friends.

Creative Expression

When I'm not coding, you'll find me engaging with STEAM pop-up events, sharing my passion for technology with others, or unwinding with my guitar. These creative outlets complement my technical work and keep me inspired.
